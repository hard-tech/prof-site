Annie and Mark talk about gender stereotypes So this image is the symbol for the man and the symbol for the woman surrounded by labels that we tend to give people as gender stereotypes, which is the idea that we label certain genders based on what people with maybe prejudice or just stereotypical ideas about what certain genders represent. Like the man is strong and the woman is beautiful. Do you agree with some of these labels, do they traditionally line up?


Yeah, definitely, traditionally they line up. Man is a money maker, the woman is sensitive, the man is adventurous, the woman is tidy, etc.

Those role models we have as kids, unfortunately they can limit us a bit. When I was growing up the kids focussed on G.I Joe if you were a boy. The idea of a tough guy, a man’s man…Tough it out, boys don’t cry. Which is a shame when you think about it, because it really limits you from expressing yourself.


And on the other side we have the Barbie girl, the girly girl. When you think about that too, right now, when you think about the gender stereotypes that we have…


When I was a child… I don’t remember having role models in film characters or TV personalities. I do remember looking up to people in my family. For example, I looked up to my dad a lot, I liked the music that he listened to. And I think that children need to have role models that they can look up to because they build their identity and they start to find out who they are partly through role models.
